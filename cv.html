Title of Thesis: Genome diversity in Triticum aestivum (bread wheat)
The aims of my PhD research are to identify and characterize genome diversity in Triticum aestivum (bread wheat). This research will establish a process for the identification of large numbers of single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) and other genetic variations in Triticum aestivum. Wheat transcriptome 454 sequence data has been applied for SNP discovery using a modified autoSNPdb method, which integrates SNP and gene annotation information with a graphical viewer. A novel algorithm SGSautoSNP has been applied for the discovery of large numbers of genomic SNPs across 16 Australian diverse bread wheat varieties. SNP density analysis provides insights into the evolution and breeding history of this important crop.
Supervisors: Prof David Edwards, Associate Prof Jacqueline Batley, Dr Jiri Stiller.
