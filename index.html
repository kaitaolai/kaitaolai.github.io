I enjoy bioinformatics!

Bioinformatics uses knowledge based on strong foundations in biology, computer science and mathematics (includes artificial intelligence and machine learning) to process biological data and develops methods deriving from a wide variety of computational techniques. I have learned great depth of multidisciplinary knowledge from bachelor of science in applied chemistry, dual masters in information technology and advanced mathematics majors. I work in bioinformatics that applies multidisciplinary knowledge and enjoy bioinformatics research!

My CV
I am working in bioinformatics research.

I am postdoctoral research fellow in bioinformatics at the Transformational Bioinformatics Team in Australian eHealth Research Centre, CSIRO. My work involves research projects in the broad area of bioinformatics that is relevant to gene editing.

My expertise is in genome informatics, high throughput genomic data analysis, computational genome engineering, gene editing, machine learning as well as big data analysis and elastic cloud computing. I have involved the projects in the pangenome analysis to investigate the gene diversity and genome evolution of commercially and ecologically important species, the genome variation analysis using next-generation sequencing data for genomic selection of important crops, and the development of bioinformatics approaches for the analysis of microbial genomic, metagenomic, or meta-transcriptomic data.
RECENT PROJECT

My current work is focusing on the development of predictors for CRISPR-Cas12a (AsCas12a) in gene editing by training a Random Forests (RF) machine learning method, and facilitating the adaptation of the latest developments in computing infrastructure (e.g. Spark for big data analytics) and cloud technology (e.g. AWS Lambda) for research projects and commercial software products.
