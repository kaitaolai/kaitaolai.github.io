Genetic variation across 16 Australian bread wheat varieties
We have applied a novel algorithm SGSautoSNP for the discovery of large numbers of genomic SNPs across 16 Australian diverse bread wheat varieties. More than 10x whole genome shotgun Illumina paired read sequence data was generated through a bioplatforms collaboration and the data mapped to the draft assemblies of chromosomes 7A, 7B and 7D. This identified more than 3 million SNPs with a validated accuracy of 93%. SNP density varied along the lengths chromosome syntenic builds as well as between genomes.
SNP density analysis provides insights into the evolution and breeding history of this important crop. Furthermore, this SNP resource permits the application of high resolution skim based genotyping by sequencing, trait association and analysis of structural variation in populations. All SNPs are publically available on a GBrowse genome viewer at www.wheatgenome.info.
WheatGenome.info
We provide a variety of web-based systems hosting wheat genome and genomic data to support wheat research and crop improvement. WheatGenome.info is an integrated database resource which includes multiple web-based applications. These in- clude a GBrowse2-based wheat genome viewer with BLAST search portal, TAGdb for searching wheat second-generation genome sequence data, wheat autoSNPdb, links to wheat genetic maps using CMap and CMap3D, and a wheat genome Wiki to allow interaction between diverse wheat genome sequencing activities. This system includes links to a variety of wheat genome resources hosted at other re- search organizations. This integrated database aims to accel- erate wheat genome research and is freely accessible via the web interface at www.wheatgenome.info. More details can be found here.
Wheat autoSNPdb
AutoSNPdb has been successfully applied to identify SNPs from Sanger sequence data for several species, including barley, rice and Brassica, but the volume of data required to accurately call SNPs in the complex genome of wheat has prevented its application to this important crop. DNA sequencing technology has been revolutionized by the introduction of next-generation sequencing, and it is now possible to generate several million sequence reads in a timely and cost-effective manner. We have produced wheat transcriptome sequence data using 454 sequencing technology and applied this for SNP discovery using a modified autoSNPdb method, which integrates SNP and gene annotation information with a graphical viewer. A total of 4 694 141 sequence reads from three bread wheat varieties were assembled to identify a total of 38 928 candidate SNPs. Each SNP is within an assembly complete with annotation, enabling the selection of polymorphism within genes of interest. It is available online at here. The online demonstration has been published on YouTube. More details can be found here.
Differential homeologous gene expression in Triticum aestivum
Gene conservation is related to the sequence and timing of polyploidisation and possibly selection by breeders. The 7D genome of Triticum aestivum is founded with more functional genes because it is preferentially conserved. It is preferentially expressed. It has fewer SNPs because of a result of selection and fixation by breeders. Breeders need to focus on getting more variation into the D genome. I have done the detection of expressed wheat genes by alignment of Chinese Spring ESTs with wheat group 7 (including 7A, 7B, 7D syntenic builds, Group 7 extra contigs and 4AL genome), and the process of detecting the best-matching locations for ESTs on Group 7A, B and D. The differential gene expression of conserved homoeolgous genes have been analysed through comparison of the Group 7A, B and D genomes.
